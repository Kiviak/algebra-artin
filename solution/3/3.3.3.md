suppose there exists $a,b,c\in \R$ such that $ax^2+b\cos (x)+ce^x=0$. let $x=0,\pi/2,-\pi/2$, then
$\begin{bmatrix}
   0 & 1 & 1  \\
   \pi ^2/4 & 0 & e^{\pi/2}  \\
   \pi ^2/4 & 0 & e^{-\pi/2}
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
   a \\
   b \\
   c
\end{bmatrix}=0$
we have $a=b=c=0$. thus, it is clear $x^2$, $cos(x)$, and $e^x$ are linearly independent.
