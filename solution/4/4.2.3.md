let
$A=\begin{bmatrix}
   a_{1,1} & a_{1,2}  \\
   a_{2,1} & a_{2,2} \\
\end{bmatrix}$
if $A$ carries the line $y=x$ to the line $y=3x$, then for any $p\in\R$ we have
$\begin{bmatrix}
   a_{1,1} & a_{1,2}  \\
   a_{2,1} & a_{2,2} \\
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
   p  \\
   p  \\
\end{bmatrix}=
\begin{bmatrix}
   (a_{1,1}+a_{1,2})p   \\
   (a_{2,1}+a_{2,2})p  \\
\end{bmatrix}$
is a point of the line $y=3x$, then $(a_{2,1}+a_{2,2})p=3(a_{1,1}+a_{1,2})p$. we have $a_{2,1}+a_{2,2}=3(a_{1,1}+a_{1,2})$ and $a_{1,1}+a_{1,2}\not=0$.
